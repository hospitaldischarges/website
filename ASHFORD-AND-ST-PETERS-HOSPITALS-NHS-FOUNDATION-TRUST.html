            Hospitals in Ashford And St Peter’s Hospitals NHS Foundation Trust unable to discharge more than four in every five patients

            In Ashford And St Peter’s Hospitals NHS Foundation Trust between April 1 2022 and May 31 2023 on an average night 89.2% patients who had been identified as ready to be discharged that day were still occupying a bed at midnight.

            This is 31 percentage points higher than the average figure for trusts across England (58.2%).

            On 13 days 100% of patients classed as “no longer meeting the criteria” to be in hospital were still occupying beds at midnight. This means that on 3% of days for which data was provided, not a single patient was discharged.

            The day when the most patients were left in hospital was Thursday November 17 2022 when 225 who could have been discharged were still in the hospital at the end of the day.

            The proportion of patients ready to be discharged but still occupying beds was worst on Sundays, when 90% of patients identified as ready to be discharged that day were still occupying a bed at midnight.
